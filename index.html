Biography
  • Sojourner Truth was born into a slavery family as Isabella Baumfree in 1797 .
  • She was sold for one hundred dollars to a sadistic master when she was nine years old.
  • Then at a profit to a fisherman, and finally in 1810, to the Isaac Dumont family with whom she stayed until the fall of 1827.
While with Dumont
  • She met and fell in love with a slave named Robert from a neighboring farm around 1815. But their relationship was forbidden by Robert ‘s owner Catton.
  • she was given as wife to Thomas, who had had two wives sold away from him before.
  • Then she had five children, four girls and one boy.
The Spirit Calls Me
  • In 1843, she changed her name to Sojourner Truth and became a Mehtodist.
  • Since then , Truth took part in social activities to fight for slavery rights and women’s rights.
Death
  • On November 26,1883, Truth died at her home in Battle Creek, Michigan.
  • More than 3,000 people pay their last respects to the black heroine.

Contribution
About the women`s vote rights
  •   She was one of the first people to make the connection between the rights of slaves and black people and the rights of women.
  •   And she was the first African American woman to make public speeches about it.
A writer let the public know about the black`s life
  •   The Narrative of Sojourner Truth: A Northern Slave'.
  •   This was one of the first accounts ever published of the life of a female slave, and the book was a success.
A feminist
  •   Sojourner was in the abolitionist
  •   She said: 'There is a great deal of stir about coloured men getting their rights but not a word about the coloured women theirs. You see, the coloured men will be masters over the women, and it will be just as bad as it was before.’
  •   Insisting that black and poor women were ‘women too’, and that they had to be included in any vision of social reform.
A campaigner
  •   Raise money for African American Civil War soldiers, worked as a nurse and taught domestic skills to freed female slaves.
  •   Be active in the women's movement, and also in relief associations for freed slaves
  •   She started and fought the campaign that ended segregated public transport in Washington
